Religious Schools

The Islamic Foundation of Toronto’s religious schools are committed to providing students with excellent Islamic education according to the Qur’an and Sunnah, based on high moral and ethical values in an Islamic environment.

We seek to develop a positive Muslim identity in each student, nurturing their spiritual, physical, and intellectual growth for success in today's world while upholding true Islamic values and establishing a strong commitment to their faith, family, community, and humanity.

Full-Time &
Evening Hifz

Our full-time and evening Hifz programs are designed to teach students to memorize the Qur’an under the guidance of qualified and experienced teachers according to the tenets of tajweed and tarteel to ensure accuracy and fluency. The Hifz programs combine the spiritual significance of committing the Qur'an to heart with rigorous discipline and academic focus.

Beyond memorization, the programs also emphasize understanding the Qur'an’s meanings and teachings, nurturing not only a strong memory but also a deep appreciation for Allah’s message. By completing Hifz, students carry a lifelong bond with the Qur'an and a unique role as its guardians and conveyors.

Evening Madrassah
& Sunday School

The evening school is committed to providing students with foundational Islamic knowledge to foster spiritual growth, community cohesion, and a deeper understanding of faith. Lessons cover a wide range of topics, from learning how to recite Qur’an properly to studying the fundamentals of Islamic belief (aqidah), knowledge of how to practice Islam (fiqh) and the seerah of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

Qualified teachers, often trained scholars or experienced community leaders, emphasize the importance of ethics, compassion, and unity as core tenets of Islamic education. Moreover, the schools teaches mutual respect and understanding, ensuring that students are not only educated in Islam but also prepared to be active, positive contributors to wider society.
